Besides Science I enjoy reading history,mythology and also anything remotely related to science in general as well. I have listed some reviews of books which I throughly enjoyed diving into.

  • What is Life? - Erwin Schrodinger

This is the take on Biology of one of the pioneers of Quantum Theory for the lay reader. The book is divided into two main parts the first of which ranks amongst the best introductions to the ideas of genetics and evolution. Schrodinger aptly describes how non-trivial the existense of DNA molecule is on the basis of its so high entropy. In the second part tipped more on the philosophical side, he discusses the relation between biology and consciousness.

  • Adventures of a Mathematician - Stanislaw Ulam

This is the autobiography of one of the finest mathematicians of the 20th century. The author has expressed the life of a pure mathematician right from being a student to the end. Occasionaly we can find tidbits of history and personal annecdotes with people ranging from Neumann to Feynmann. Personally I found the anecdote of Neumann on atleast they lie on a plane to be a literal ROFL moment!

Read it for a glimpse on life of a mathematician and for its cheerfulness/nastiness!
